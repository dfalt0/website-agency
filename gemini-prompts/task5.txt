Since we’ve dialed in the detection logic, the Migration Blueprint needs to feel like a high-value, bespoke document—not just a list of results. This is the moment where you transition from "AI Scanner" to "Human Concierge."

Here is the design for the Migration Blueprint results view. It’s structured to highlight "Pain Points" and "The Upgrade Path" based on what the scanner found.

1. The Blueprint Layout
The UI should be split into a "Current State" (Red/Neutral) and an "Optimized State" (Emerald).

2. Strategy: The "Audit Conclusion"
To make this work as a sales tool, you want the user to feel like they are reading an exclusive internal memo.

The "Rating": Give them a score (e.g., 64%). People hate being "below average" and will want to reach out to see how to get to 100%.

The "Engineer's Note": Use simple conditional logic to write a "note" that mentions their specific tech. It feels like you (the human) are talking to them, even if it's automated.

The "Blueprint": Use words like Infrastructure, Hardened, Edge Scaling, and Zero-Downtime. These are professional buzzwords that justify a high-end price point.
